## 1. Product Summary

Yoora Sarah is building a premium digital commerce ecosystem for modest fashion that combines:

- elegant browsing and storytelling
- conversion-safe assisted shopping
- omnichannel customer support
- internal operational intelligence
- AI copilots for both buyers and internal teams

The product is not a single page or feature. It is a connected experience made of:

- customer webstore
- social and assisted commerce journeys
- internal management and SDM portal
- data, workflow, and AI operating layer

## 2. Why This Exists Now

Yoora Sarah already shows strong brand and product-market signals:

- live owned-commerce website
- strong Instagram presence and active product storytelling
- category breadth across fashion, accessories, and kids
- visible use of assisted selling and launch education

This creates a clear next step:

- move from channel-led execution to system-led execution
- move from manual guidance to AI-assisted guidance
- move from reactive operations to forecasted, measurable operations

If Yoora Sarah does not formalize this now, the likely outcome is:

- channel growth without operating clarity
- rising service workload
- slower launch execution
- fragmented customer data
- AI experiments that are disconnected from business outcomes

## 3. Product Vision

Create the most trusted premium modest fashion buying experience in its category, powered by elegant UX, expert guidance, and AI systems that make both shopping and operations feel calm, intelligent, and efficient.

## 4. Product Principles

1. Premium first.
2. Modesty is not a niche afterthought; it is the core design language.
3. Guided discovery beats overwhelming choice.
4. AI must reduce friction, not add novelty noise.
5. Human warmth must remain visible in every critical journey.
6. Every workflow must have measurable business value.
7. Existing approved UI/UX is the execution baseline; delivery should refine and extend it, not replace it.

## 5. Opportunity Statement

Yoora Sarah can increase margin quality, conversion quality, and internal throughput by owning three advantages:

- stronger owned-commerce conversion through better trust, guidance, and assisted checkout
- higher buyer confidence through styling and size intelligence
- faster internal execution through AI-assisted planning, content, and production decision support

The business operating model should be explicit:

- the public-facing website is for buyers and consumers
- the internal dashboard is for management and SDM or employee workflows
- both surfaces must stay separated in interface and access, but synchronized through shared data, workflow, and AI services

## 6. Primary Personas

### Persona A: Yooya Core Buyer

- Women shopping for modestwear with high preference for elegance, comfort, and syar'i suitability
- Needs confidence in styling, fit, fabric feel, and purchase safety
- Often influenced by Instagram storytelling and visual curation

### Persona B: Assisted Buyer

- Interested buyer who still needs human reassurance before paying
- Often asks via WhatsApp or DM about size, stock, launch timing, or checkout process
- Very important for conversion recovery and preorder flows

### Persona C: Repeat Collector

- Existing customer who waits for new shades, new drops, restocks, or edit releases
- Responds well to personalized launches and continuity across collections

### Persona D: Internal Manager / Owner

- Needs fast clarity on launches, performance, approvals, bottlenecks, and priorities
- Cannot rely on fragmented spreadsheets and chat-based updates

### Persona E: Marketing / CS / Production Team

- Needs a single operational system with less manual repetition
- Benefits from guidance, summaries, automation, and structured approvals

## 7. Jobs To Be Done

### Buyer JTBD

- Help me discover products that fit my style and occasion without wasting time.
- Help me understand whether a piece will suit my body, use case, and wardrobe.
- Help me checkout with confidence, especially for launch or preorder items.
- Help me keep track of what is new, restocked, or worth buying next.

### Internal JTBD

- Help me launch collections faster with less coordination overhead.
- Help me write, review, and approve content with better quality and consistency.
- Help me forecast demand, stock, and size mix with better confidence.
- Help me keep the whole team aligned without re-explaining everything manually.

## 8. Problem Statements

### Customer problems

- Product discovery can still depend too heavily on manual browsing.
- Size and fit hesitation can block conversion.
- Assisted-selling channels may close sales, but they are expensive to scale manually.
- Launches and preorders create urgency, but also confusion if the buyer lacks timely guidance.

### Internal problems

- Product, content, forecast, and production decisions may live in separate mental models.
- Approval flow can become slow when leadership context is not summarized well.
- Valuable customer insight from website, WhatsApp, and Instagram can be lost if not structured.
- Teams spend too much effort on repetitive drafting, reporting, and clarification work.

## 9. Goals

### 12-month product goals

- Make the official website the most trusted conversion channel.
- Reduce buyer hesitation through AI-guided discovery and fit support.
- Turn the portal into the operational center for management, SDM, briefs, forecast, production, approvals, and reporting.
- Establish safe, measurable AI systems for buyer support and internal execution.

### Success metrics

- Increase conversion rate on the owned website.
- Increase AOV through bundles, sets, and look-based merchandising.
- Increase repeat purchase rate and launch participation.
- Reduce average assisted-selling response time.
- Reduce design-to-launch and approval cycle time internally.
- Improve forecast accuracy and reduce stock inefficiency.

## 10. Product Scope

### In scope

- premium customer webstore experience
- search, browse, category discovery, and storytelling
- product detail, size guidance, recommendations, and bundles
- cart, checkout, order tracking, and profile
- WhatsApp and support integration
- AI stylist and support assistant
- internal portal for management, SDM, briefs, design jobs, patterns, forecast, approvals, and planning
- internal AI copilots for management, SDM, design, content, planning, and decision summaries
- reporting, audit, and governance foundations

### Out of scope for the first major delivery wave

- standalone native mobile app
- full multi-tenant SaaS architecture
- public external partner APIs
- generative design automation without human review
- autonomous pricing without managerial approval

## 11. Experience Vision

The digital experience should feel:

- quiet, refined, and expensive
- highly intentional in copy, spacing, and motion
- editorial in taste but operationally practical
- capable of moving seamlessly from inspiration to assisted purchase

Execution guardrail:

- the current website UI/UX is already considered aligned to the live Yoora Sarah brand expression
- implementation should preserve the existing visual language, layout character, and navigation patterns unless a later decision explicitly approves redesign
- product work should focus on content accuracy, trust signals, AI capability, operational flow, and component-level refinement inside the current experience direction

The portal should feel:

- clear
- role-aware
- non-chaotic
- measurable
- decision-oriented

## 13. Risks and Assumptions

### Risks

- AI recommendations may sound generic if product data is weak.
- Size guidance may create liability if the logic is not clearly framed.
- Teams may resist workflow change if portal usage adds friction.
- Marketplace and social channels may still fragment the customer journey.

### Assumptions

- Yoora Sarah will continue to invest in owned-commerce growth.
- Management wants the website and portal to operate as one business system.
- AI adoption is intended to improve quality and speed, not replace brand taste.
